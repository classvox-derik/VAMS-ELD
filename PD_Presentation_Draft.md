## Section 1: Introduction to VAMS-ELD (5 Minutes)

### Slide 1: Welcome to VAMS-ELD

- **Slide Title:** Empowering Every Learner with VAMS-ELD
- **Bullet Points:**
  - Introducing the English Language Development Scaffolding Platform.
  - Designed specifically for Valor Academy Middle School.
  - Bridging the gap between grade-level content and language proficiency.
- **Speaker Notes:** "Good morning, everyone! Today we are diving into VAMS-ELD. This platform was custom-built to solve a challenge we face every single day: how to make complex, grade-level academic content accessible to our English Learners without 'dumbing it down.' We know that manual scaffolding—writing different word banks, creating sentence frames, and chunking text for three different proficiency levels—can take hours of planning time. VAMS-ELD uses advanced AI to automate that process, giving you precision tools to support your students' linguistic growth in seconds rather than hours."

### Slide 2: The Problem & The AI Solution

- **Slide Title:** From Manual Labor to Precision Support
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **The Challenge:** Time-consuming manual differentiation for Emerging, Expanding, and Bridging tiers.
  - **The Solution:** AI-driven scaffolding that preserves original content rigor.
  - **The Result:** Instant, tiered instructional layers for every student in your roster.
- **Speaker Notes:** "As ELD teachers, we are required to provide Integrated and Designated support. The traditional workflow involves a lot of 'copy-pasting' and manual formatting. VAMS-ELD changes the equation. Instead of simplifying the text itself, the AI layers on supports—like color coding and visual organizers—that act as 'training wheels' for the language. Our goal is intellectual quality and meaningful access. The system is designed to preserve ALL original assignment content; the AI only adds HTML-formatted supports around or alongside your text to ensure it remains standard-aligned and rigorous."

## Section 2: Account Setup & Roster Management (10 Minutes)

### Slide 3: Step 1: Secure Sign-Up

- **Slide Title:** Joining the Community: Teacher Sign-Up
- **Bullet Points:**
  - Navigate to `vams-eld.vercel.app` (or your local instance).
  - **Requirement:** You MUST use your `@brightstarschools.org` email.
  - **Verification:** Check your inbox for the activation link.
  - **Personalization:** Set your name in Settings for a customized dashboard.
- **Speaker Notes:** "Let's get everyone on-boarded. Please navigate to the sign-up page. A critical technical detail: our system uses a strict 'Domain Guard.' The codebase enforces a check that only permits @brightstarschools.org email addresses. If you try to use a personal Gmail, the system's middleware will automatically block access to protect student data. After you sign up, check your school email for a confirmation link. Once you're in, I encourage you to visit the Settings page to add your first and last name—the dashboard uses this data to greet you personally each morning!"

### Slide 4: Navigating the Student Roster

- **Slide Title:** Roster Management: Data-Driven Instruction
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Centralized View:** Access all students via the sidebar.
  - **Key Fields:** SSID, Grade (5-8), and Primary Language.
  - **Homerooms:** Organized by college names (USC, UCLA, CSUN).
  - **ELPAC Data:** Track Overall Level, as well as Oral and Written scores.
- **Speaker Notes:** "The heart of the system is the Roster Management screen. This is where we track our students' linguistic profiles. For every student, you'll see their SSID and grade level. We also organize students by their homerooms, named after colleges like USC, UCLA, and CSUN. Most importantly, the platform tracks specific ELPAC proficiency data: the student's Overall level, plus their Oral and Written scores. This granularity is vital; for instance, a student might be a 'Level 3' in speaking but a 'Level 1' in writing. Seeing this allows you to choose exactly which scaffolds they need for a specific task."

### Slide 5: Understanding Proficiency Tiers

- **Slide Title:** Tiered Support: Emerging, Expanding, Bridging
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Emerging (Amber):** Substantial support for basic communicative needs.
  - **Expanding (Orange):** Moderate support for applying language in complex contexts.
  - **Bridging (Green):** Light support for students approaching grade-level proficiency.
- **Speaker Notes:** "VAMS-ELD uses the California three-tier proficiency model, color-coded for ease of use. Emerging students get 'Substantial' support—think short chunks and simple definitions. Expanding students get 'Moderate' support, focusing on academic language and word maps. Bridging students are nearly independent and receive 'Light' support, focusing on nuanced vocabulary and sophisticated analysis frames. These tiers are built into the UI as badges (Amber, Orange, and Green), making it easy to see at a glance how your roster is distributed."

## Section 3: Demo - AI Assignment Scaffolding (15 Minutes)

### Slide 6: The 3-Step Workflow

- **Slide Title:** Creating Your First Scaffolded Assignment
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Step 1: Input Source.** Paste text, upload a file, or import from Google Docs/Slides.
  - **Step 2: Details.** Add a Title, Subject, and Grade Level for context.
  - **Step 3: Select & Generate.** Choose students and apply specific scaffolds.
- **Speaker Notes:** "Now for the demo. Creating an assignment is a simple 3-step process. First, you provide the content. You can paste text directly, upload a document, or even just import from Google Docs. Step 2 is adding details; giving the AI a 'Subject' (like Science or ELA) helps it provide better, more relevant vocabulary definitions. Finally, in Step 3, you select your students. You can 'batch select' by EL level—for example, selecting all 'Emerging' students in your UCLA homeroom—and then choose which of the five scaffolding tools you want to apply."

### Slide 7: The 5 AI Scaffolding Strategies

- **Slide Title:** Precision Tools for Language Development
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Color Coding:** Highlights main ideas, evidence, and transitions.
  - **Chunking:** Breaks text into manageable sections with headers.
  - **Sentence Frames:** Tiered starters for opinion and summary writing.
  - **Word Banks:** 6-12 academic words with level-appropriate definitions.
  - **Visual Organizers:** Adds structured maps (e.g., Venn diagrams).
- **Speaker Notes:** "The AI doesn't just guess; it follows specific instructional strategies. Color Coding highlights topic sentences in yellow and evidence in green. Chunking inserts section headers to prevent cognitive overload. Sentence Frames are automatically inserted before questions—Emerging students might see 'The main idea is...', while Bridging students see 'While some argue X, the evidence suggests Y.' Word Banks are perhaps the most powerful: the AI writes simple definitions for Emerging students and nuanced, academic definitions for Bridging students. Finally, Visual Organizers provide structured spaces for students to map out their thinking."

### Slide 8: Reviewing the Output

- **Slide Title:** Tabbed Results & Teacher Guidance
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Tabbed Results:** View different levels in a single window during batch generation.
  - **Teacher Instructions Card:** AI-generated advice on how to use the scaffolds.
  - **Word Bank Review:** Check the terms and definitions before sharing.
- **Speaker Notes:** "When you click 'Generate,' you'll see the results in a specialized viewer. If you generated versions for multiple EL levels at once, they appear in tabs at the top. Each result includes a 'Teacher Instructions' card. This is a guide from the AI explaining why it chose certain frames or words for that level. You can also review the generated Word Bank at the bottom of the assignment to ensure the definitions are exactly what you want your students to learn."

### Slide 9: Exporting to Google Docs & PDF

- **Slide Title:** Seamless Integration with Your Classroom
- **Bullet Points:**
  - **Print Ready:** One-click 'Download PDF' for physical handouts.
  - **Digital Ready:** 'Export to Google Docs' for Google Classroom.
  - **Persistence:** All assignments are auto-saved to your personal Library.
- **Speaker Notes:** "Once you're happy with the assignment, you have two main export options. You can download a clean, formatted PDF that is ready for the printer. Alternatively, you can click 'Export to Google Docs.' This creates a brand-new file in your Google Drive with all the colors and formatting intact, making it perfect for digital submission. Every single assignment you create is also saved in your Library, so you can search for it by student name or title and reuse it next year."
